Thrasher Covers

A quick overview of all the Thrasher covers from 1981 to 2022

By Rupert Pupkin / July 2022

Since Burnett already got mad at me for it, I'll admit that I don't read the mag very often but I've always enjoyed the big pictures on the front. The cover of Thrasher has always seemed like a pretty good measure of what the big dogs are up to, and assuming skate 'milestones' (like Jaws' 25 stair ollie) are covered by covers, it seems like you could get a pretty clean picture of the progression of skating by looking at whatever tricks made it for any given year.


obstacle frequency

Pretty interesting watching the decline of transition in the early days and the rise of everything else. Shoutout to 2021 for being the first year since the mid 90's to not have a handrail cover.



trick frequency

Kickflip and tre flip are the only flip tricks in the top 20, and out of 519 covers there are only 14 flips into grinds/slides and 0 flips out. I guess flipping in/out of tricks for a photo is probably more trouble than its worth. Save it for the video part brother nobody cares.


staircount by year

This is the highest stair count of each year split into handrails, hubbas and tricks down stairs. Hover over any of the points to see what the cover was and click to get a closer look.



Rails
The stair count for rails plateaus around the start of the 2000's. People traded out bigger rails for better rails and it got progressively harder to get a rail cover without a kink, curve, gap or drop. Felipe Nunes' cover is the only rail outlier under the trend line but he's lightyears above the trendline for people with no legs. Steve Nesser deserves an honourable mention for grinding a 51 rail and crunching the whole chart. (But two 50's down two 9's is gangster).

Stairs
Couldn't imagine a more understandable plateau. If people kept trying to skate bigger stairs there's going to be a point where the impact would give everyone flamingo knees like Jaws. I guess there aren't a lot of tricks that look good down stairs in photos so until some freak ollies a 30 I doubt we'll see much stair coverage.
